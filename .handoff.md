## 2. COMPLETED (this session ŌĆö PR-3 slice 2: the per-town supply solve)

User said "continue" ŌåÆ the economy track's headline next step was **PR-3 slice 2** (the per-town
supply solve), and every prerequisite was in place (the storage swap #294, slice 1's cargo on
routes, and all four C1 extractions ŌĆö `tickIntermediateGoods` lives in `systems/goods.ts`). So
this session wrote slice 2 **there**, in `systems/goods.ts`, not the monolith.

- **The mechanic.** Slice 1 solved the goods chain ONCE on the nation aggregate (`produceGood`
  deposits `baseOutput┬Ęlevel` split by sector weight; `drawGood` drains `level` of each input from
  one nation-wide pool). Slice 2 keeps that nation-wide cascade driving **every macro signal**
  (`supplyChainHealth`, `supplyShockSeverity`, the output drag `supplyShockMult`, the pharma/
  electronics secondary RNG effects) ŌĆö byte-identical ŌĆö but resolves the **STOCK LEDGER per town**:
  new `distributeGoodProduction(r, good, level)` makes each town's sector-weighted share of
  `baseOutput┬Ęlevel` **only to the extent the town locally HOLDS that good's INTERMEDIATE inputs**
  (Liebig gate `min(1, have/need)`), and consumes those inputs from the town's **own** ledger
  (`shipGoodFrom` debit, `addGoodStock` deposit). Raws stay folded into `level` (the sector proxy),
  so they never gate per-town.
- **Why single-town play is byte-identical.** A lone town produces every input it consumes (stocks
  grow unbounded), so every gate is 1 ŌåÆ it makes the full `baseOutput┬Ęlevel`, exactly as the old
  pool did. The loop runs in **catalog order, which is topological** (every intermediate input
  precedes its consumer ŌĆö now guarded by a test), so a town's own upstream output *this tick* is in
  stock before its downstream goods read it ŌĆö parity holds even at an unlock boundary
  (clothing+textiles 1920, machinery+components 1930). A second invariant also guards it:
  `baseOutput(i) Ōēź #consumers(i)` for every input, so the first-tick gate is 1 before any buffer
  accrues (also now a test).
- **What diverges (the intended change).** A **cross-sector** good in a **multi-town** nation:
  `clothing`/`consumer_goods`/`luxury_goods` are industry-attributed yet need agri `textiles`. A
  pure-industry town holds no textiles ŌåÆ makes none of them; the textiles sit **stranded** in the
  agri town (verified: consumer_goods 0, textiles 10 in the agri town) ŌĆö until textiles are
  **shipped in** (the gate reads current stock incl. arrived cargo ŌåÆ production restored). Mixed
  (self-sufficient) towns don't diverge.
- **Macro-neutral TODAY ŌĆö and that's correct.** Nothing in the economy reads good-stock
  *magnitudes* yet (the cascade reads `rawSupplyLevel` for raws only, never a tracked good's
  amount), so the per-town ledger divergence does **not** reach GDP/inflation/etc. Proven: the
  **headless 8-seed ├Ś 181y markers are byte-identical to base**; the divergence is purely in the
  serialized per-town `goodStocks`. The playtest-worthy balance shift arrives with the **next**
  slice (per-good local prices that read local stock). So slice 2 ships with **zero balance risk** ŌĆö
  it's the substrate, fully landed.
- **Gates.** tsc clean; **988 tests** (980 + 8 new in `tests/goods-local.test.ts`); determinism
  harness Ō£ģ (exercises multi-town centuries ŌĆö determinism, not equivalence-to-base, is the gate
